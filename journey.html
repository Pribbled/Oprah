Why She Chose Entrepreneurship

Oprah sought control over her content and financial independence. Instead of being just a host, she became a media owner—producing, directing, and distributing her own content.​

Founding Harpo Productions

In 1986, she established Harpo (Oprah spelled backwards), making her one of the few entertainers to own their production company. This move shifted the power from the networks to herself.

Highlights:

  • Negotiated full ownership of The Oprah Winfrey Show
  • Produced successful films like The Color Purple and Selma
  • Created space for diverse voices and stories

Transition to OWN Network

In 2011, Oprah co-founded the Oprah Winfrey Network with Discovery. Although it had a rocky start, OWN eventually became a hub for empowerment-driven content.

Key Business Moves

  • Built multiple revenue streams: television, publishing, online, wellness
  • Used her brand to influence product sales and public opinion (e.g., Oprah’s Favorite Things)
  • Collaborated with Apple TV+ and WeightWatchers as a board member and shareholder
